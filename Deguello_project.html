About the Project

Degüello is a turn-based tactical RPG with a strong narrative focus. Players will control Azael and his group members during phase-based strategic battles (planning and execution) on grid-divided scenarios. Numerous stealth sections add variety to the gameplay.


Most Important Implementations


Combat:

The combat in this project stands out due to our attempt to innovate on the traditional formula of tactical games. To achieve this, we designed a system where, instead of choosing an action for each unit individually, the player must plan five actions for all units while considering the enemy's reactions to these moves. Once the actions are selected, the player advances the round, executing the queued actions for all units on the map simultaneously.

Below is a detailed sequence of actions represented in the attached images, essential for understanding the game process in question:

  • In the first image, labeled with the number one (1), the battlefield is shown, including the movements the opponent will make before the player executes any actions. Next, the section marked with the number two (2) presents all the options available to the player during their turn. These options include: moving, attacking, using an item, employing a skill, or waiting.



  • In the second image, labeled with the number three (3), the actions the player has planned during the planning phase are shown, prior to moving on to the execution phase. It also highlights how the enemy's behavior adapts in real-time based on the player's decisions.



  • Subsequently, the player proceeds to the execution phase, where the planned actions are carried out in an orderly and simultaneous manner (4), alongside the opponent's behavior.



  • Finally, in the image labeled with the number five (5), the confrontation between the two characters is illustrated, taking into account all the actions executed by both. Additionally, in number six (6), the health status of the player-controlled character is displayed, while in number seven (7), the enemy's health is shown.



The implementation of this entire system was a long and complex process. Additionally, since there weren’t many references to games attempting a similar mechanic, numerous unforeseen issues arose. Despite this, the system ended up working even better than we had anticipated, and I hope to revisit it someday—though next time, it will definitely be in Unreal Engine.



Graphics and Effects:

During the development of this project, I was still learning the fundamentals of graphical programming, so this aspect doesn’t include major demonstrations of my skill. Even so, I’d like to highlight three instances that I find interesting:

  • Grass: I created these grass segments using a geometry shader that optimized resources. This shader required two textures: one to determine where the grass should be displayed (which could be based on just one channel) and another to provide the color for the different segments created, achieving the style we were aiming for.



  • Water: The water was created specifically for this scene, but I saw it as an interesting exercise. The caustics and the depth effect allowed me to learn a lot about how rendering works.



  • Dithering: Given the view we were trying to implement, many objects caused issues by obstructing important elements like units. To resolve this, I developed a dither effect that was applied exclusively to these key elements.
